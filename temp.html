Spatial microsimulation allows analysis of multiple levels of data in a single modelling framework. So far we have used (and generated) data at individual and geographical levels. The next most important level of human organisation — since the decline of the tribe — is the household (Wheelock and Oughton, 1996). Households are worth studying as self-standing units because many phenomena, including where to live, access to resources (financial, intellectual and physical — such as cars and bicycles) and friendship networks, operate at this level.
